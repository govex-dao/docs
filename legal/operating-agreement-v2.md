**DEFINITIONS**

For the purposes of this Agreement:

**"Distributed Ledger System"** means a decentralized digital infrastructure that enables the secure, transparent, and immutable recording of transactions or data across a distributed network of computers (nodes). It operates as a shared ledger using cryptographic techniques, ensuring trust without relying on central authorities.

**"Company Accounts"** means the accounts or Smart Contracts at the following addresses on the Sui Network: the DAO Contract address specified in Article V.1, and any other addresses determined from time to time to hold property of the Company by the Futarchic Management System or the Member.

**"Futarchic Management System"** or **"Futarchy"** means Govex's onchain algorithmic governance framework that implements futarchy as a decision-making methodology, using market-based conditional mechanisms to determine organizational outcomes. Under this system, proposed actions are evaluated through conditional markets, and the action associated with the market outcome reflecting the highest predicted organizational value shall be deemed approved and is contractually binding, implemented automatically via Smart Contract or pursuant to the Company's governance procedures.

**"Governance Token"** or **"$GOVEX"** means the token at address 0x0f5a49f57d89b812eface201194381d5c81b462f39b90b220a024750737ea5d4::govex::GOVEX on the Sui Network. Holding the Governance Token confers Market Participant status but does not confer membership, ownership, or any economic interest in the Company.

**"Market Participant"** means any person or entity that holds Governance Tokens and interacts with the Futarchic Management System. Market Participants are not members of the Company and have no ownership interest, economic rights, governance rights beyond market participation, or fiduciary protections.

**"Smart Contract"** means any executable bytecode persistently stored on a Distributed Ledger System for operation by validators, sequencers, or other Distributed Ledger System nodes.

**ARTICLE II**

**PURPOSES AND POWERS**

II.1 **Purposes of the Company.** The Company is organized exclusively for the purpose of offering governance infrastructure as a service to communities, companies, and online networks. Its core product is a software platform that enables users to create, manage, and participate in decision-making processes using market-based governance mechanisms.

II.2 **Authority of the Company.** The Company shall have and exercise all of the powers and authority conferred by Marshall Islands law on an entity recognized as a decentralized autonomous organization limited liability company.

II.3 **Assets and Other Property.** All assets, tokens, and other property that comes under the possession or control of the Company Accounts shall automatically be deemed to be owned by, and constitute the sole property of, the Company, except to the extent otherwise expressly determined by the Futarchic Management System or the Member.

II.4 **No Distributions to Market Participants.** The Company shall not distribute dividends, profits, or other economic returns to Market Participants. Market Participants have no economic interest in the Company and no expectation of profit from the Company's operations or the efforts of others.

**MEMBERSHIP**

III.1 **Sole Member.** The Company shall have a single Member who is the sole owner of the Company and holds 100% of the membership interest. The name of the Member is set forth in **Exhibit A**.

**ARTICLE IV**

**MARKET PARTICIPANTS**

IV.1 **Status of Market Participants.** Persons or entities holding Governance Tokens are Market Participants. Market Participants are expressly **NOT** members of the Company. Holding Governance Tokens does not confer any:

(a) Membership interest or ownership stake in the Company;
(b) Economic rights, profit-sharing rights, or rights to distributions;
(c) Voting rights in the Company (distinct from market trading);
(d) Fiduciary duties owed to them by the Company or Member;
(e) Rights upon dissolution of the Company;
(f) Standing to bring derivative actions on behalf of the Company.

IV.2 **Nature of Market Participation.** Market Participants interact with the Futarchic Management System by trading in conditional markets and submitting proposals. This participation constitutes use of a protocol, not investment in or ownership of the Company. Any gains or losses from market trading result from the Market Participant's own trading activity, not from the efforts of the Company or its Member.

IV.3 **No Reliance.** Market Participants acknowledge that they have no expectation of profit derived from the efforts of the Member or the Company. Market Participants participate at their own risk and are responsible for their own trading decisions.

IV.4 **Disclaimer of Fiduciary Duties.** To the maximum extent permitted by law, the Company and the Member owe no fiduciary duties to Market Participants. Market Participants' sole rights are those arising from their interaction with the Smart Contracts comprising the Futarchic Management System.

IV.5 **Restricted Persons.** It is the policy of the Company to comply with applicable sanctions regimes. Persons subject to United Nations targeted financial sanctions or residents of embargoed regions (Iran, Syria, Cuba, North Korea, Crimea, Donetsk, Luhansk) are prohibited from being Market Participants.

**ARTICLE V**

**MANAGEMENT**

The Company is contractually bound to implement proposals approved through the Futarchic Management System in the manner specified in the approved proposal.

V.2 **Member Emergency Powers.** Notwithstanding V.1, the Member retains the right but not the obligation to:

(a) Make decisions concerning the business affairs of the Company when:
   1. The Algorithmic Management is unable to function due to technical failures, bugs, or edge cases;
   2. A decision is required for interfacing with legacy or offline institutions;
   3. Immediate action is necessary to protect the Company from material harm;
   4. Implementation of a Futarchy outcome would require offchain actions that violate applicable law in any relevant jurisdiction. The Member shall refuse to implement any such offchain actions. Onchain actions executed by the Smart Contracts are final.

(b) Until the 28th of March 2026, disable new proposal creation without prior notice. After waiting for any live proposals to finalize or by giving four days of notice, whichever is shorter, update any smart contract addresses, package IDs, or other technical identifiers referenced in this Agreement, when necessary to:
   1. Address security vulnerabilities or bugs;
   2. Maintain operational continuity;
   3. Implement technical improvements.

Such updates shall be documented and communicated through official venues, with the Agreement deemed automatically amended to reflect new identifiers after four days have passed.

V.3 **Authority to Bind Company.** The Futarchic Management System, the Member, and authorized agents of the Company shall have the authority to bind the Company. Market Participants, in their individual capacity, have no authority to bind the Company.

V.4 **Compensation.** The Member may be compensated for services to the Company as determined via the Futarchic Management System.

**ARTICLE XI**

**DISPUTE RESOLUTION**

XI.1 **Futarchy Dispute Resolution.** Any controversy, dispute, or claim arising out of or relating to this Operating Agreement, the Company, the Futarchic Management System, or any action or omission relating thereto (a **"Dispute"**) shall first be submitted for resolution through the Futarchic Management System. Futarchy-based resolution is a condition precedent to any arbitration or judicial proceeding.

XI.2 **Notice of Dispute.** If a Dispute is not resolved through the Futarchic Management System within thirty (30) days, the party asserting the Dispute shall provide written notice to the Company (the **"Notice of Dispute"**). The parties shall attempt in good faith to resolve the Dispute during the thirty (30) day period following service of the Notice of Dispute.

XI.3 **Arbitration.** If the Dispute has not been resolved within sixty (60) days after the initial submission to the Futarchic Management System, the Dispute shall be finally settled by arbitration administered by the International Centre for Dispute Resolution in accordance with its International Arbitration Rules then in effect. The arbitration shall be seated in the Republic of the Marshall Islands, governed by Marshall Islands law, and conducted in English. The arbitrator's award shall be final and binding.
